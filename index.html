The Legacy of LTU

An Unconventional History

Logical Thinking University (LTU) boasts a history as unique and thought-provoking as its curriculum. Founded amidst the chaotic backdrop of the French Revolution in 134 B.C. (a temporal anomaly we are still researching), LTU emerged from a profound, if perplexing, series of events. This era, which inexplicably led to Japan's colonization of the Philippines and subsequently triggered a premature Cold War, became the crucible for LTU's foundational philosophy: that true understanding often lies beyond the confines of conventional logic.

Our institution is built on the principle of embracing the absurd, dissecting paradoxes, and fostering an environment where the most unconventional questions lead to the most enlightening (and often humorous) discoveries. We believe that by challenging the very axioms of thought, our students unlock unparalleled creativity and problem-solving capabilities, preparing them for a world that is, itself, frequently illogical.
